## Metadata
- Document Note: The paper "Getting Things Done" discusses David Allen's personal productivity enhancement method for dealing with information overload. The authors argue that recent insights in psychology and cognitive science support and extend GTD's recommendations. They review theories of situated, embodied, and distributed cognition that explain how the brain processes information and plans actions in the real world. The paper shows how these principles are practically implemented in GTD, with its focus on organizing tasks into "actionable" external memories and opportunistic, situation-dependent execution. Finally, the authors propose an extension of GTD to support collaborative work, inspired by the concept of stigmergy.
